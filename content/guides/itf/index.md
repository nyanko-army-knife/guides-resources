#### Disclaimers

- This guide primarily focuses on Into the Future, but most of what is here can also be applied to early-mid game in general.
- This guide is an unofficial sequel to [Lucas' Beginner's guide](https://youtu.be/9ATc_PTYVzA). If you haven't watched it, it is recommended you do, as multiple aspects Lucas talked about in his video will be brought up here as well.
- If you're confused by any terminology or acronyms here, remember to refer to the Terminology guide on this website. 
- While this guide provides multiple in-game tips and pieces of advice, it does not go in depth on game mechanics.

Assuming you have just finished EoC Chapter 1, you may have seen a new section of stages called "Into the Future" (ItF for short). You may have even tried the first or second levels within it. If you have, you will see cyan-colored enemies: Aliens. As you may have noticed, Alien enemies are really strong. Even the first stage's Shibalien has about as much health and damage as a 400% Hippoe, and as ItF 1 progresses you will encounter further stronger Aliens with extremely high stats. For this reason, it is recommended to only try ItF once you have completed EoC 3 and obtained full Superior treasures in each chapter. This is beneficial in a few ways: 
- Treasures, as you may remember from the EoC guide, are really important! 
- Clearing EoC 2 increases the maximum level cap of your units from 10 to 20, which is an incredibly large buff to your units. 
- Clearing EoC 3 gives you Bahamut Cat, a ridiculously powerful unit if you don't have any Uber Rares. 
## ItF Treasures

Speaking of Treasures, not only are they really important, but ItF's Treasure sets are different from the ones in EoC. The main notable difference is the existence of Fruit and Crystal Treasures. 
Fruits increase the quality of anti-Trait abilities, such as the length of status effects, or damage dealt from the Massive Damage multiplier. The sets available in ItF include the Void Fruit (for Dark enemies), Blood Fruit (for Red enemies), Sky Fruit (for Floating enemies), and Heaven's Fruit (for Angel enemies). 

Here are the exact changes in multipliers once all treasures are collected :

| Ability                     | Without treasures                         | With maxed out (300%) fruit treasures     |
| --------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- |
| Slow, Freeze, Weaken, Dodge | -                                         | +20% duration                             |
| Knockback                   | -                                         | +30% distance                             |
| Strong Against              | 1.5× damage dealt 0.5× damage taken (1/2) | 1.8× damage dealt 0.4× damage taken (2/5) |
| Massive Damage              | 3× damage dealt                           | 4× damage dealt                           |
| Insane Damage               | 5× damage dealt                           | 6× damage dealt                           |
| Resistant                   | 0.25× damage taken (1/4)                  | 0.2× damage taken (1/5)                   |
| Insane Resist               | 0.166× damage taken (1/6)                 | 0.142× damage taken (1/7)                 |
ItF does not include Fruits for Zombie or Alien enemies, as those are found in Cats of the Cosmos (CotC), the next set of story mode chapters. As of tihs writing, there are no Fruit Treasures for Traitless, Relic, or Aku. 

The other Treasure to worry about are the anti-Alien Crystals. There are two Crystal Treasure sets per chapter. From the beginning, Alien enemies have a base magnification of 700%, and every fully completed Crystal reduces this number by 100%, eventually bringing the default Alien magnification to 100%. This magnification is multiplicative as well, meaning that a 200% Alien enemy would have an effective magnification of 1400% if no Crystals were completed (200% x 700%.) For this reason, the Crystals are the most important Treasures to obtain in ItF as they make huge differences in stats. Collect them as soon as possible and get them to be fully Superior, especially if you become stuck on Alien-heavy stages. 

## When should I start ItF?

Most players tend to repeatedly get stuck when playing through ItF, and a lot of the time, it’s because their unit levels are very lackluster. Why? Because playing through ItF alone gives you very little XP, and no Leaderships at all. For that reason, it’s better to start playing through some beginner Event stages, early Catclaw Championship stages, as well as to collect all of the EoC Treasures before starting ItF, as this gives you a ton of resources to upgrade your units and progress. Stories of Legend (referred to in-game as the Legend Stages) also gives plenty of XP to work with as well. 

While this is more of an opinion than something you should precisely follow, here's an example of what a good progression could look like: 

- EoC 1 - 3
- ItF Ch. I
- SoL 1 - 12
- ItF Ch. II
- SoL 13 - 18
- ItF Ch. III

While SoL chapters do have higher Crown difficulties, you shouldn't do them this early, for reasons explained later on in this article. 

## Map layout, Timed Score rewards, and Outbreaks

The map layout in ItF also differs; the stages, or countries, aren’t in the same order, Hawaii and the Maldives are absent, and there are two new stages: The Great Abyss and Floating Continent. This doesn’t really impact gameplay though.

Something new you'll encounter in ItF are Timed Score rewards. After clearing any given ItF stage, you're provided with a score depending on how fast you cleared the level. A high enough score nets you a reward, such as a battle item, XP or Cat Food. Rewards and score thresholds vary from one stage to the next. While all this free stuff is certainly very nice, you shouldn't worry about it too much at first, especially since some of those rewards can be very hard to obtain early on.

The same thing goes for ItF Outbreaks: don’t bother trying to beat these until after completing all three chapters.

## Difficulty and Strategy

Compared to EoC levels, ItF levels tend to be somewhat more complicated, and also significantly more difficult. Losing to harder stages on your first try is to be expected, especially if you go in blind. Even though a few defeats now and then are perfectly normal, it is important to understand _why_ they occur. There are three main possible causes:

1. You are lacking treasures. Go back and collect them to have an easier time.
2. Your cats are underleveled. Take a while to level them before trying again.
3. You are using the wrong lineup or the wrong strategy.

Every time you lose, try to rethink what you did and what went wrong.
_(Disclaimer: the following is an oversimplification)_

- Were the enemies pushing through your meatshields like there weren't any? Try using more meatshields.
- Did you run out of money? Try stalling for longer before starting the fight if the stage lets you do so.
- Were all your units getting outranged? Try bringing a longer ranged cat, etc.

Essentially, don’t give up after a single failed run.

The main sources you should look at are PackPack, Dracobot, and the Battle Cats Wiki.

On the other hand, when it comes to reliable guides and strategies, YouTube is usually the best way to go. ## Normal Cat True Forms

While you're progressing through ItF, ideally you'd want to obtain some of your Normal Cats' True Forms. Of course this is RNG dependent, but you can get tickets from Catclaw Championship and log in every day to try and speed up the process. Here are some details on each of them:

- **Mohawk Cat**, the True Form of the Basic Cat, doubles its stats. Nothing really changes with this True Form as the basic Cat already had very low stats. 
- **Eraser Cat**, the True Form of Tank Cat, doubles its health. Eraser will be one of your staple meatshields for ItF, CotC, and SoL, and only becomes outclassed later on in the game. You can do without it for a bit, but the earlier you happen to obtain Eraser, the better.
- **Dark Cat**, the True Form of Axe Cat, doubles its stats. This increase simply is not enough to fix Axe Cat's main issues, being its high cost for a meatshield and poor stats and range for an "attacker". 
- **Macho Leg Cat**, the True Form of Gross Cat, doubles its stats, increases its cooldown considerably, and grants it Strong vs. Alien. For a player just starting ItF, Macho Leg can be a pretty decent way to deal with pesky Aliens like Maawth or LeMurr. However, as soon as you obtain real units that can deal damage like Paris Cat or Crazed Gross, there is no longer any need for Macho Leg Cat, as its stats are way too low for its cost.
- **Lion Cat**, the True Form of Cow Cat, nearly doubles its stats but also increases its cooldown. Cow was already unusable for all uses except for rushing very easy stages, and Lion does nothing to improve it further. Once you obtain Crazed Cow, all uses for Lion go out the window.
- **The Flying Cat**, the True Form of Bird Cat, doubles its stats, slightly increases its cooldown, and gives it a low chance to Weaken Angels. While Bird Cat may have been good in Empire of Cats for its spammability and damage, Bird quickly becomes useless due to its poor health and range, and ultimately Bird is replaceable by units such as Paris Cat or, later, Courier Cat. Flying Cat's Weaken vs. Angels is also basically useless as it occurs too little and is too weak to be considered anything more than a cool bonus. 
- **Island Cat**, the True Form of Fish Cat, doubles its stats and cooldown, and gives it an irrelevant 2% chance to land a Critical Hit. This True Form simply reinforces Fish's usage while choosing not to fix any of his issues. Island's high stats can seem impressive at first, but over time Island's poor stats will cause it to quickly fall off. Ultimately, Island is outclassed by Crazed Fish, who is not only very strong but is also very easy to obtain. 
- **King Dragon Cat**, the True Form of Lizard Cat, doubles its stats and cooldown. Dragon stacking is a powerful technique that shreds through stages which you may have discovered, and this True Form is a neat buff to the strategy but isn't exactly very important to get overall. Again, King Dragon is completely outclassed by its Crazed counterpart, as well as units like Medalist or Polevaulter. 
- **Jamiera** Cat, the True Form of Titan Cat, doubles its stats and cooldown, as well as giving it a chance to Knockback non-Metal enemies. The stat boost is very nice for Jamiera, who was already a good unit by EoC standards, but the cooldown increase severely hurts him. As for the Knockback, it's way too unreliable for you to actually use it seriously. As you progress through the game, you'll discover that tanker units like Jamiera are worse than simple meatshielding plus backliner strategies, so over time Jamiera will quickly fall off. 

In general, you don't severely *need* the Normal Cat True Forms to clear Into the Future, but the sooner you get them done the better your account will be overall. The Worker Cat upgrades, or the "blue techs" as some people refer to them as, are extremely vital, and are massive buffs to your account, so you should still try to log in often and do your Catclaw Championships to try and max them out as soon as possible. 
## Bahamut Cat

While most early game non-gacha units are pretty bad, you'll still want to level up some of them, one of them being Bahamut Cat. Bahamut is the only non-gacha powerful AoE backliner you'll obtian for a while, so you should at least understand how he works. Bahamut's main characteristics are his high range and huge damage per hit, but a ridiculously long attack rate (20 seconds!), combined with low health and a long cooldown. Despite this flaws, Bahamut is absolutely vital for the early game, as he is basically the "glue" for Dragon stacking. Dragon only has 400 range, so he can still get hit if enemies like Teacher Bear push. Bahamut, on the other hand, has high enough range to ignore this, and his massive nuke damage allows him to one shot pesky enemies without worrying about them staying alive. While Bahamut's flaws make him decently replaceable by Ubers for the most part, Bahamut is not only free but also has 450 range, which many Ubers in the game lack. 
## Gacha

The EoC guide already covered some notable units to roll, so there's not much point going over it again. Instead, what might be more useful is a quick recommendation of recommended banners. 

At this stage in the game, your best banners to roll are the Almighties and Dark Heroes banners.

Almighties has a huge amount of beneficial Ubers, with even the "worst" one, Thunder God Zeus, still having some decent generalist potential as well as having Resistant vs. Angels. Most of the units are either usable generally or are powerful in their niche, and almost all of them will be carrying you from the moment you obtain them until the end of the game. As a bonus, most Ubers here do not need their True Form to operate with the exception of Aset (and even then, she's still pretty viable.)

Dark Heroes is a banner more suited towards beating Aliens, but unlike the other anti-Alien banner in the game, Elemental Pixies, the Dark Heroes are not solely limited towards their anti-Alien niche. While most units here do not crush Aliens as hard compared to the Pixies who all have Resistant vs. Aliens as well as Barrier Breaker and Warp Blocker (abilities more suited for Cats of the Cosmos), the Dark Heroes are able to switch forms and gain different abilties, which makes them incredibly versatile. For example, Hayabusa in his first form has Critical Hit, while his second form has Slow and Knockback vs. Aliens. His True Form, on the other hand, targets Zombies instead. While none of the Dark Heroes are truly alike and thus have their effectiveness vs. Aliens vary wildly, basically everything you roll will be good enough to beat ItF with somewhat relative ease. 

As a reminder, don't roll 150 draws ever, and don't burn Rare Tickets on non-fest banners. Only roll on both of these banners solely with Cat Food, and only when a Guaranteed event is on. If you want to know about what to roll in the future, you can go to the r/battlecats Discord server and check the \#gacha_advice channel for help. 

## Crazed Cats

Even if you have most strong gacha units, you'll still want to clear the Crazed Cat stages, as the units you get from them are truly powerful. 

In case you don't feel like checking YouTube or other resources for exact strategies on each stage, here's a quick summary of every stage: 

- **Crazed Cat (Dark Souls)**: Pretty straightforward, stacking Bahamut can do the trick as long as you know how to stall. Make sure you have 4 meatshields, though
- **Crazed Tank (Absolute Defense)**: Endurance stage. You should have 5 meatshield optimally to do this one. Pretty annoying and lengthy, but is still fairly easy with Bahamut and, if you have them, spammable midrangers
- **Crazed Axe (Berserk)**: Easily the hardest Crazed stage. The boss attacks fast and has an annoying Freeze effect, supported by endless waves of Red enemies with strong pushing power and high stats. Do this one last.
- **Crazed Gross (Festival Gross)**: Pretty difficult. Since Crazed Gross unleashes powerful Wave attacks, the way to beat this one is to just not meatshield and to instead use tankers and ranged attackers. This one can be a pain, unless you have Awakened Bahamut or Octopus. Either of these makes this one free.
- **Crazed Cow (Head Shaker)**: A test of how much DPS you can unleash. 
- **Crazed Bird (Flappy Cat)**: Another endurance stage. Not really that difficult overall, though you should do this one after Crazed Tank. 
- **Crazed Fish (Fish Hell)**: Fairly easy using meatshields, Dragon, and Bahamut, save for the boss: Super Metal Hippoe. It outranges both Jurassic Cat Sitter and Moneko, so you must obtain Space Cat from the Red Cyclone before you do this one. (Unless you have Ubers.)
- **Crazed Lizard (Mammals?)**: Either use tankers, or rush this one with Awakened Bahamut
- **Crazed Titan (Crazy Cats)**: Stack ranged attackers and get lucky.

While you don't need all of these immediately, the main ones to go for are Cat, Tank, and Fish. The other ones are good (for the most part), but they're not quite as vital. Apart from them all having higher stats overall, here's a quick look at the differences between the Normal and Crazed Cats: 

- **Cat:** The Basic Cat, but faster. 
- **Tank**: Tank Cat, but...faster...
- **Axe**: Just a better Axe Cat, though still suffers from the same problems (low range, high cost).
- **Gross**: Able to create Wave attacks, but has a much longer cooldown.
- **Cow**: Cow Cat, but much faster and with Area Attack
- **Bird**: Higher range, allowing it to counter a lot of stuff normal Bird couldn't Still has pretty short range, but is overall pretty good when given the opportunity. 
- **Fish**: Fish Cat, but better. Also faster and cheaper. While this doesn't sound like much, a levelled Crazed Fish has huge stats and will go crazy on ItF. Also needed for Behemoth Culling (more on that later).
- **Lizard**: Lizard, but better. 
- **Titan**: Compared to Jamiera, has no Knockback but gains a 10% Wave (as well as higher speed). Can be better sometimes, but normal Jamiera is overall better (except if it's really low levelled), since it attacks faster.

## Farming and managing XP

Simply going through ItF and grinding Treasures is usually good enough, but if you're really lacking, the best place to get XP is in the XP Stage, appearing twice per day and being available all day during weekends. XP Megablitz is also an option, but it's very difficult if you don't have good anti-Darks. Another option is to play Stories of Legend for XP. Again, try to follow the XP tips mentioned in the EoC article. 

## User Rank, Catfruit and Gacha True Forms

While upgrading your units, you might have noticed how you can only upgrade them up to a certain point. Higher level caps are unlocked from User Rank.

| User Rank | Rarity      | Level cap |
| --------- | ----------- | --------- |
| 900       | Rare        | 25        |
| 1000      | Super Rare  | 25        |
| 1100      | Uber Rare   | 25        |
| 1110      | Legend Rare | 25        |
| 1200      | Rare        | 30        |
| 1300      | Super Rare  | 30        |
| 1400      | Uber Rare   | 30        |
| 1410      | Legend Rare | 30        |
| 1600      | Special     | 30        |
It should be obvious why having higher levels on units is good, so we'll gloss over that. Once you get level 30 units, you now gain the chance to True Form your gacha units. Evolving units to their True Form needs XP and Catfruit, the amount of which varie sfrom unit to unit and between different rarities. 

Catfruit is obtained from the stage called Growing Catfruit, unlocked after clearing EoC 3. This stage is always on. To obtain a fruit of a specific color, just do the stage with the color in its name. These stages aren't too difficult, being balanced around EoC 3 difficulty, so if you have Treasures you should be good to go. For recommended True Forms, there's a whole article about them, so there's not much point to talking about them. What might be important, however is the fact that each Catfruit stage costs 200 Energy to do, so it'll quickly eat up your spare Energy. If you are about to go to sleep, for example, and have noticed you haven't used any Energy yet, you can always burn that excess on Catfruit stages. 

## Stories of Legend, Ururun and Event Stages

Focusing on ItF is not a bad idea, but you might also want to try your hand at Stories of Legend (see [When should I start ItF?](#When%20should%20I%20start%20ItF?) )and some event stages.

While Bahamut is very cool, his flaws are pretty apparent. If you don't like Bahamut much, SoL subchapter 18 (Jail Break Tunnel), has a 30% chance to drop Ururun Wolf. Ururun is similar to Bahamut, but attacks much faster in exchange for lower damage per hit. This doesn't really matter, as the sheer amount of DPS (damage per second) Ururun has over Bahamut makes her arguably better than Bahamut for general purposes. While you don't need Ururun for ItF (especially if you have an Uber with over 450 range that actually deals some kind of damage), she does make it much easier. 

Event stages also exist. Since there are a ton of stages, the main thing you should do is to look at the star rating. If it's low enough, give it a try. Some of these maps are also time limited and drop important units/True Forms, so try to get as many as possible if they're easy enough. 

Out of these stages, some of them stand out for being very crucial. For example, the Cyclone stages. These stages feature the Cyclones as bosses: imposing enemies with huge health and damage. However, these enemies have very low range (for the most part) and speed, so they are extremely susceptible to crowd-control abilities, most of which you can find from the Rare Cat Capsule as Rares and Super Rares. These units also drop certain units at a 30% chance, all of which are oriented towards Metals in some way. In particular, you'll need Space Cat from the Red Cyclone to clear Crazed Fish. 

Another set of stages are the Behemoth Culling stages. There's a lot to discuss about them, so check out their own article for information about them. 
### Enigma Stages

Another thing to note: once you get Ururun, _Enigma Maps_ are now unlocked. After clearing any Legend or event stage, there is a chance to get an Enigma Map based on how much Energy you have spent since the last Enigma drop. These come in three different rarities, with each rarity containing differing sets of stages. The chance of getting higher rarity maps will increase as you unlock more SoL-exclusive units, which is referred to in-game as your Enigma level. Because Enigma Stages come from spending Energy, and furthermore because Hunter's Map stages are tied to your Enigma level, you should only do the bulk of SoL content on higher crown difficulty once you have unlocked Hunter's Map III. Because you'll be spending huge amounts of energy to get SoL done with, all of this will be converted into Enigma maps. While this doesn't mean you're forbidden from doing the super early SoL maps on 3 and 4 Crown (in fact, you should for more XP), save the bulk of it for later.
